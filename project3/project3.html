Responsive Web Design & Google Analytics

Explain the importance of responsive design and its impact on the target audience. (minimum 50 words).

Responsive Web Design allows webpages to be viewed without issues on all devices and platforms. Developers are able to use HTML and CSS to resize a webpage. They can also hide certain components. Without responsive web design you will experience layout cutoff's inconsistent sizing, and more. Webpages that are responsive gives users the freedom of viewing webpages from anywhere without visibility and accessibility issues.

Google Analytics provides a wealth of data for free to help assess the success of websites. Describe three different kinds of reports that will tell you what information your target audience is accessing (minimum 100 words).

Site Speed Overview Report

The Site Speed Overview Report tells you which webpages have a long load time, and why. Webpages that load are least likely to be viewed repeatedly. Slow loading pages also decrease the engagement of the specific page.

Navigation Summary

The Navigation Summary Report allows developers to see what navigation path user take when viewing the site. The reports allow developers to see where users come from, how they get through your site, and where they are going afterwards.

Mobile Overview Summary

The Mobile Overview Report helps developers locate where the “un-mobile friendly” sections are in their site. The pages that are not mobile friendly will decrease engagement and viewership of the site.
